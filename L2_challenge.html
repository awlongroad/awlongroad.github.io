Design Your Own Operating System

A single-lesson research challenge

Choose a real-world scenario and design the perfect operating system for it

Link to Your Learning

Remember what you learnt about the three types of operating systems:

Network OS - manages networks and shared resources

Open OS - source code is free and can be modified

Proprietary OS - owned by companies, costs money to use

Today you'll decide which type works best for a specific real-world situation!

Your Challenge

You're going to design a brand new operating system for a specific situation where normal operating systems like Windows, macOS, or Linux wouldn't work very well.

Step 1: Choose Your Scenario

Pick ONE scenario below that interests you:

Smart School

A school where all classrooms, lights, heating, and security are controlled by computers. Students use tablets that share work instantly.

Hospital Emergency Room

Medical equipment, patient records, and communication systems that must work 24/7 without ever failing or crashing.

Space Station

Computers on a space station that control life support, experiments, and communication with Earth with very limited internet.

Gaming Caf√©

20 gaming computers that need to run the latest games perfectly, share files, and never slow down during busy times.

Elderly Care Home

Simple systems for elderly residents to video call family, monitor health, and call for help easily with big buttons and clear displays.

Delivery Drone Network

Hundreds of drones delivering packages that need to avoid each other, find addresses, and work in all weather conditions.

Why These Scenarios Need Special Operating Systems

Normal operating systems like Windows or macOS are designed for general use. These special situations need:

  • Different levels of security
  • Different ways of sharing information
  • Different user interfaces
  • Different reliability requirements

Step 2: Design Your Operating System

Answer these questions about your chosen scenario:

  1. What type of OS will you choose?
    Will it be Network, Open, or Proprietary? Explain why this type is best for your scenario.
  2. What are the main problems to solve?
    What doesn't work well with normal operating systems in your scenario?
  3. What special features does your OS need?
    Think about security, speed, sharing, user interface, or reliability.
  4. Who will use your OS?
    Describe the people who will use it and what they need it to do.
  5. What could go wrong?
    What happens if your OS crashes, loses internet, or gets hacked?

Example: Smart School OS

OS Type: Network OS - because it needs to connect all classrooms and share resources

Problems: Normal OS can't control lights and heating, students' work doesn't sync between devices

Special Features: Controls building systems, instant file sharing, parental controls, educational apps

Need Help Thinking of Ideas?

  • What devices need to be connected together?
  • Does it need to work without internet?
  • How important is it that it never crashes?
  • Do different people need different levels of access?
  • Does it need to be very secure or very simple to use?

Step 3: Create Your Design Document

Write up your operating system design. You can use Word, PowerPoint, or just write it by hand.

What to Include:

Writing Tips

  • Use simple, clear sentences
  • Give specific examples rather than general statements
  • Think about real problems that actually exist
  • Your diagram can be hand-drawn - it doesn't need to be fancy!
